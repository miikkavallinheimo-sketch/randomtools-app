D&D Dice Roller with Advantage and Modifiers

This professional D&D dice roller supports all polyhedral dice types (d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20, d100) with advantage/disadvantage mechanics, modifiers, and automatic critical detection for D&D 5e.

Features for Dungeon Masters and Players

  • Advantage/Disadvantage: Roll twice and automatically take the higher or lower result
  • Modifiers: Add ability scores, proficiency bonuses, and situational modifiers
  • Critical Detection: Automatic highlighting of natural 20s and natural 1s
  • Quick Presets: One-click access to common D&D rolls like attacks, damage, and initiative
  • All Dice Types: Complete set of RPG dice from d4 to d100
How to Use for D&D

Select your dice type (d20 for most rolls), choose normal/advantage/disadvantage, add any modifiers, and roll. The result shows both the dice roll and final total. Perfect for:

  • Attack rolls with advantage
  • Ability checks with modifiers
  • Damage rolls for spells and weapons
  • Initiative and saving throws
D&D 5e Mechanics

This roller follows D&D 5th Edition rules:

  • Advantage: Roll two d20s, use the higher result
  • Disadvantage: Roll two d20s, use the lower result
  • Critical Hit: Natural 20 on attack rolls
  • Critical Failure: Natural 1 on attack rolls and death saves
D&D Dice Types

Each die in D&D has a specific shape and use:

  • d4 (Tetrahedron): A four-sided pyramid shape. Used for daggers, darts, and minor spells.
  • d6 (Cube): A classic six-sided die. Used for short swords, firebolt, and many basic weapons.
  • d8 (Octahedron): Eight-sided diamond shape. Used for longswords, healing spells, and divine smite.
  • d10 (Pentagonal Trapezohedron): Ten-sided die. Used for battleaxes and damage calculation.
  • d12 (Regular Dodecahedron): Twelve-sided die. Used for greataxes and heavy hitting weapons.
  • d20 (Regular Icosahedron): Twenty-sided die. Used for all attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws.
  • d100 (Percentile): Combines two d10s to generate numbers 1-100. Used for random tables and percentages.
